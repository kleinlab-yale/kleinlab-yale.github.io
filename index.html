Welcome to the KLAY, (Klein Lab at Yale).

Modeling Biology, Molding Therapeutics.

KLAY studies the structural basis of oncogenic signaling and builds biologic therapeutics for invasive cancers through integrated translational research.

Research

Focus areas

Current directions highlighted from lab updates and active programs.

Receptor Tyrosine Kinases (ROS1, ALK, ErbB3)

We determine how oncogenic receptor tyrosine kinases are activated, regulated, and hijacked in cancer, using structure-guided approaches to design next-generation therapeutic antibodies and biologics.

Isthmin-1 (ISM1)

We investigate ISM1 as a newly recognized, insulin-like metabolic hormone, defining its structure, receptors, and mechanisms of action in metabolic regulation and disease.

MCAM (Melanoma Cell Adhesion Molecule)

We study how MCAM controls cell adhesion and migration, uncovering context-dependent mechanisms that regulate tumor invasion and metastasis.

LEG1

We explore LEG1 as a metabolic stress-responsive hormone, elucidating how it coordinates cellular adaptation to nutrient and energetic states.
